INTERNET INFRASTRUCTURE

Home Infrastructure

ETHERNET CABLE

In order to connect everything, ethernet cables are used for a wired internet connection between devices - often going from a modem/router to the device.

MODEMS & ROUTERS

The main hub for the home's internet, this connects up to the main underground internet wire and modulates digital info transmissions to be recieved by the modem wherever the website is based. Routers are an additional hub connected to the modem as an extension for more ethernet plugs.

WiFi EXTENDERS

Travelling through the copper wires (and sometimes other wires) in the house's power lines, this can be plugged into any power plug socket to extend the wifi signal from the modem.

City Infrastructure

TRAIN SCHEDULE

Providing a live changing screen, these train schedules display upcoming trains constantly updated via the internet through the local wires at the station, most likely getting the info from a data center for Metlink.

INTERNET WIRES (GRATE)

This grate in particular is a Telecom (Spark) grate for access to the internet wire lines that travel beneath the city. Many of these are spread across Wellington, both Telecom and other internet providers.

WiFi ZONES

These are old phone booths remodelled to advertise local free wifi zones, accessable by anyone. These use modems to connect to the aforementioned internet wires.

Data Centres

DATACOM

One of the two major data centres in Wellington, holding up to 300 racks of servers used for website hosting, domains and traffic. Located 68-86 Jervois Quay.

XTREME NETWORKS

Another major data center, located 191 Thorndon Quay. Their site doesn't specify total racks but provides much more information on their site.
